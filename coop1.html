WORKTERM 1 & 2 Report

A reflection of my time at the University of Guelph’s Computing and Communication Services as a Student IT Consultant

About CCS

The CCS department is made up of several teams that work together to provide IT services for the University of Guelph and its Guelph-Humber and Ridgetown Campuses. The department manages everything from supporting the network infrastructure of the campus to managing all the login accounts for staff, faculty, students, retirees, etc. Some of the teams include Software Distribution, Security, Networking, Web Solutions, Managed Servers, Managed Desktops, and IT Help Services which is where I worked.

Job Description

Over the last 8 months, I was on the front lines of IT support for the University of Guelph, helping clients such as staff, faculty, and student. Part of the week I was at the IT desk in our library, where people could come visit us in person. Frequently, I had to help people with problems singing in with their university login, software installations/troubleshooting, malware protection, networking issues, slow computers and many other individual issues like helping someone use a software or recovering documents.

On the other days of the week I was at the Help Centre, troubleshooting technical issues with clients over the phone, on our online chat, and on an email ticketing system called "Footprints". Additionally to that I was communicating with internal teams such as Managed Desktops, Networking infrastructure, Info. Security, to escalate information on technical issues and incidents.

Many of the skill I needed for the job such and clear communication, thinking on my feet and troubleshooting had to be developed on the job because everyday was a new day with their own challenges. Although, I had the opportunity to use my web development experience to do project work and update webpages used by our entire team to access links and document.

Goals & Skills

Working at CCS was an amazing opportunity for me to grow and learn new skills that will benefit me in future work experiences.

My communication skills have improved so much over the past 8 months. When I first started working, I was not at all confident working with clients and often wouldn’t know the right questions to ask or ways to layout steps for troubleshooting. Though with experience and learning from my mistakes I have become much more comfortable with interacting with clients, following up with them to make sure their problem was solved, and escalating information to my team members to help find solutions collaboratively.

I also developed my teamwork and leadership skills while working within my team in CCS. Since the experience I got was largely dependent on the types of issues I encountered, I learned to collaborate with my team and share knowledge on how to troubleshoot specific issue and work together to find solutions. Towards the end I even trained some of the new staff on procedures and troubleshooting steps to take for issues that frequently came up.

Problem solving was a big part of my job. Each client’s issues came with its own set of challenges, over my work terms I developed my ability to quickly think on my feet and inquire and analyze the situation. I did research on my own on how to troubleshoot common computer issues that I was seeing often, and it gave me the tools and the confidence to solve more and more complicated issues.

This was an incredible opportunity and I has so much fun working at CCS. Huge thanks to my Supervisor Jill Hogg for giving me the amazing experience and all my teammates for making it memorable.
